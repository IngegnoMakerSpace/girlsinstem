GirlsInSTEM

"We want people to be able to reach a point where they become independent thinkers and self sufficient builders." Fran Sanchez - Beachlab

Girls between 14 and 25 years old could join us to discover the world of digital art, reverse engineering and wearable technology.

During the project, 3 bootcamps were organized

Are you an organisation interested in obtaining extra information on the toolkits we have developed? Or do you want to support us on this road to more Girls in STEM? Let us know via mail to info@decreatievestem.be.

Results of the Project

E-textiles

The E-textiles toolkit has been released as a booklet under cc-by-nc licence. Learn how to make a fabric button, a textile stroke sensor, or a flip dot eye and more. Download our E-textiles Toolkit Booklet [21 Mb]

Digital Arts

The digital arts toolkit is divided in several parts:

  1. Learn to use a micro::bit workshop, were you learn how to create animations with the micro::bit. Download Micro::bit animation workshop manual and the slides
  2. Design your own VR glasses with our manual
  3. Learn how to make Augmented Reality (AR) Instagram filters with our workshop manual
  4. Learn how to make a digital movement instrument (DMI) using combining a Micro::bit with your instrument with our manual and slides
  5. Create a paintbot with our manual and slides.

Reverse Engineering

Learn how to scan an object to use it for reverse engineering projects. Learn how to make a smoke machine from scavenged parts. Or do you want to learn how to make a small vacuum cleaner? Everyting is described in our instructable, released under cc-by-nc-sa licence. Download the Reverse Engineering Instructable [10 Mb].

About the Project

The aim of this project, funded by the European Commission through the Erasmus+ programme, is to empower girls to pursue their interests in Science, Engineering, Technology and Maths subjects (STEM), providing them with positive examples, support and possibilities to experience STEM occupations. Through this initiative, girls will be able to realise that they can embark on a fulfilling and successful career in STEM.
